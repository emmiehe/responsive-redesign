A friend of mine used to live in a place that did not have easy access to laundry machines. He talked about this great service called "SimpleWash". But he also complained that their website was "messy", especially when he needed to change his pickup or delivery time, it would take him a few minutes to find the place to correct those info from the website.

As a student in a UI/UX CS class, I got curious about the website of SimpleWash laundry service. And I will talk about my discoveries and proposals after examining the their current homepage at simplewash.us.

Identifying Usability Problems

simplewash.us

Issue One: untamed images

When a user lands on the homepage of SimpleWash, the background image - a woman holding a "HELP" sign behind some half-folded clothes - effectively conveys the message that it is a laundry related service. While it is an impressive way to express how helpful this service could be, the high saturation of the image makes the texts and buttons floating on it very hard to read. In fact, a user could easily scroll over the most important function of this website - the "Schedule Now" button.

Issue Two: illegible texts

Besides too many high saturation images, the small font size and low text-background contrast make the texts almost illegible. This point is further verified by WebAIM WAVE: there are 27 contrast errors on the homepage.

Issue Three: redundant links

There are at least 4 different links that lead to the same scheduling page. On top of that, many blank links don't lead anywhere - it seems they should appear as texts instead of links (for example, "Eco-friendly" and "Donations gladly taken" look like links, but they are just texts).

Issue Four: overlapping content

The page has a fixed menu bar but its background is transparent, making the content of the page overlapping the menu bar when a user scrolls up. This creates a messy look visually, which I assume is not ideal for a laundry service.

Issue Five: unclear structure

The text and image grouping is not very meaningful. After scrolling over the entire page, the clearest message to me is "Share laundry day. Give & get $10!". All the other time I am bombarded with images and these images simply do not clearly depict the exact services of this business. This could potentially hurt the learnability of the website for first-time users like me.

Issue Six: lacking memorability

As my friend has pointed out, for casual users, it can be hard to remember where the "Change Delivery" button is (it is hiding under "Schedule") because the arrangement of functionalities is not intuitive.

Issue Seven: not as efficient

I guess that the original design adds redundant links to increase efficiency, but what's interesting is that not every section of the webpage can be reachable from somewhere else. For example, if an experienced user wants to "share and get $10", they know such functionality exists, but when they land on the page they will not find it at the menu bar. Instead, they will have to scroll over most of the sections to find it.

Visual Redesign

Low-fidelity Wireframing

My design idea is to follow the original one scrolling page structure, increase the simplicity of the webpage, change its text-hierarchy and color scheme, use box layout to communicate a better structure, and improve its efficiency.

I will only be designing and implementing the home page of the website, which means "Schedule", "Reschedule", "Pricing", "FAQ" will be dummy buttons.

Some annotations and comments:
  • Navbar should be fixed on top to allow easy access to each section.
  • Each section has its own div and the homepage (first section) and the contact page should have almost white background to have a clean look.
  • Each section uses flexbox to handle responsiveness: for example, the "Services" section is one flex-column containing three flex-row, the second flex-row is reversed to make the grouping appear interesting.
  • Majority of original webpage's images are replaced with simple icons to make it less distracting for a user to read.
  • The "Help" background will show up on larger devices as a fixed background cover, but will not appear on medium to small devices.
  • All forms use bootstrap form style with a btn-block submit button to allow easy clicking for users. Placeholders are not shown in the prototype but should follow the original page's place holder style to avoid confusion from existing customers.
