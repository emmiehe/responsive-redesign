A friend of mine talked about this great service called "SimpleWash", but complained that their website was "messy" - when he needed to change his pickup or delivery time, it took him a few minutes to find the correct form.

As a student in a UI/UX CS class, I got curious about the website of SimpleWash laundry service. Here I will talk about my discoveries and proposals after examining the their current homepage at simplewash.us.

Identifying Usability Problems

simplewash.us

Issue One: untamed images

The high saturation of the background image makes the texts and buttons floating on it hard to read - a user could easily scroll over the most important function of this website - the "Schedule Now" button.

Issue Two: illegible texts

The small font size and low text-background contrast make the texts almost illegible. This point is further verified by WebAIM WAVE: there are 27 contrast errors on the homepage.

Issue Three: redundant links

At least 4 different links lead to the same scheduling page. Many blank links don't lead anywhere ("Eco-friendly" and "Donations gladly taken" look like links, but they are just texts).

Issue Four: overlapping content

The page has a fixed menu bar but its background is transparent, making the content of the page overlapping the menu bar when a user scrolls up.

Issue Five: unclear structure

The text and image grouping is not very meaningful. This could potentially hurt the learnability of the website for first-time users like me.

Issue Six: lacking memorability

As my friend has pointed out, for casual users, it can be hard to locate the "Change Delivery" button (it is hiding under "Schedule") - the arrangement of functionalities is not intuitive.

Issue Seven: not as efficient

Not every section of the webpage can be reachable from somewhere else. If an experienced user wants to "share and get $10", they will have to scroll over most of the sections to find it.

Visual Redesign

Low-fidelity Wireframing

My design idea is to follow the original one scrolling page structure, increase the simplicity of the webpage, change its text-hierarchy and color scheme, use box layout to communicate a better structure, and improve its efficiency.

I will only be designing and implementing the home page of the website, which means "Schedule", "Reschedule", "Pricing", "FAQ" will be dummy buttons.

Some annotations and comments:
  • Navbar should be fixed on top to allow easy access to each section.
  • Each section has its own div and the homepage (first section) and the contact page should have almost white background to have a clean look.
  • Each section uses flexbox to handle responsiveness: for example, the "Services" section is one flex-column containing three flex-row, the second flex-row is reversed to make the grouping appear interesting.
  • Majority of original webpage's images are replaced with simple icons to make it less distracting for a user to read.
  • The "Help" background will show up on larger devices as a fixed background cover, but will not appear on medium to small devices.
  • All forms use bootstrap form style with a btn-block submit button to allow easy clicking for users. Placeholders are not shown in the prototype but should follow the original page's place holder style to avoid confusion from existing customers.
